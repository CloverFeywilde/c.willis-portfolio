Meet Bryan Willis.

I’m a Visual Designer with skills in front-end development, UI, UX, and print media. I’m also a Freelance Illustrator, and I utilize my painting and drawing skills to cultivate my design aesthetic. Honestly though, it doesn’t stop there for me. I do a lot of things and take pride in my wide skill set. For me personally, I believe too much specialization is the death of my own ingenuity and stunts my creativity. Simply put, I don’t ever settle on anything, and I’ve found during my work that in order to be successful in one area, one must also be skillful in another. My love for art and visual expression has frequently led me to hone other skills such as computer programming, photography, business management, web development, graphic design, and a host of other skills.

At the end of the day, no matter what situation I find myself in, I’m always building things and taking on multiple projects at a time. It’s never enough for me to idly sit back and make plans, I get my satisfaction from seeing the project come together, whatever it might be, and seeing the tangible results before my eyes. Whether that’s a traditional fine arts painting, a fantasy illustration, or a front end UI design for an app or website.

A common theme across my body of work is the use of the female figure as both a subject and a design element. I do this with the purpose in mind of empowerment and liberation. My liberal depictions of the female body are not only forms of artistic expression, but are also a showing of my admiration and respect for women, their struggle, and their place in our society right now. I’ve also found that the female figure is one of the most captivating forms of visual design, can serve as a striking centerpiece, and provide inventive solutions to a number of design problems. When used harmoniously with other design elements, an expressive female figure says “Look at me!” as it draws the attention of it’s viewers.
