Meet Bryan Willis.

Iam a Visual Designer with skills in front-end development, UI, UX, and print media. I’m also a Freelance Illustrator, and I utilize my painting and drawing skills to cultivate my design aesthetic. The truth is that I do a lot of things and take pride in my wide skill set. I believe too much specialization is the death of ingenuity and stunts creativity. In order to be successful in one area, I’ve found that one must also be skillful in another. My love for art and visual expression has frequently led me to hone other skills such as computer programming, photography, business management, web development, graphic design, and a host of other skills.

I get my satisfaction from seeing the project come together, and seeing the tangible results before my eyes.
No matter what situation I find myself in, I’m always building things and taking on multiple projects at a time. It’s never enough for me to idly sit back and make plans, I get my satisfaction from seeing the project come together, and seeing the tangible results before my eyes.

A common theme across my body of work is the use of the female figure as both a subject and a design element. I do this with the purpose in mind of empowerment and liberation. My depictions of the female body are expressions of my admiration and respect for women and their struggles. I’ve also found that the female figure is one of the most captivating forms of visual design, and can provide inventive solutions to a number of design problems. When used harmoniously with other design elements, an expressive female figure says “Look at me!” as it draws the attention of it’s viewers.
