The making of bwillis.info

To demonstrate a solid technical foundation, I coded and designed my website from scratch without the help of any templates or content management systems. The tools I used were Notepad ++ for coding, SASS for CSS preprocessing, Koala as a compiler, Adobe Photoshop for editing graphics, codepen for sandbox testing, and Github's software for all of my logging and backup management. I started by sketching several layouts in a small sketchbook, eventually moving to a larger pad of graph paper to flesh out a more concrete layout.

With such a broad range of skills being represented here, I wanted to give the viewer as much agency as I could.

My website was designed mostly as a scrolling 1page design, but was laid out to gradually lead the viewer's eye, and not overwhelm them with too much striking imagery at once. With such a broad range of skills being represented here, I wanted to give the viewer as much agency as I could. I coded the entire website to respond to different device's viewports and had to redesign the look of the website drastically for mobile devices and tablet displays.
