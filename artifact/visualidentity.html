My Visual Identity Development

The development of my own personal identity has been a trying journey from me, and has led me through a plethora of iterations. Just as I would with any design problem, I used this opportunity to go through an organized process and come up with a sustaining visual marker. The main challenge of this design problem was by far unity. Coming up with a single symbol to represent such a wide array of creative skills was beyond challenging. I started the daunting task by conceptualizing my identity and creative skillset. I made several list and put together collages of my past work to help provide insight. I then researched other sustaining brand identities that I found relevant to my flavor of work, creating collages and mood boards along the way. Lastly, I designed my my logo by sketching thumbnails, picking my favorites, and later moving my favorite designs onto photoshop for more variations. I further experimented with typography, and lastly color.

Coming up with a single symbol to represent such a wide array of creative skills was beyond challenging.
I chose Helvetica as my personal brand’s typeface, as it’s classically designed and when bolded it can make a strong statement. I was inspired particularly by American Apparel’s usage of typography, and how they pair bold fonts with expressive female figures to create a strong brand that isn’t afraid to speak loudly. Green I chose as my color due to how it’s unique, vibrant, and expressive. The paint splatter I felt best captivated both my unique nature and creative skills at a single glance. The orderly “B” on the inside of the splatter I designed to convey the idea of an underlying structure to the creative energy splattering freely from it’s core.
