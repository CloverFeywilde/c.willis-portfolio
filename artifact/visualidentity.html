My Visual Identity Development

Icreated my visual identity marker by working through an organized process of iteration and repetition, with the main challenge of representing multiple marketable skills with one symbol. I started by researching other sustaining identities, and then conceptualized my identity with collages. I then sketched thumbnails by hand, and moved my favorites to Photoshop.

Designing one symbol to represent a wide array of creative skills was beyond challenging.
Inspired by American Apparel’s use of thick typography paired with sensual female figures, I chose the classically designed Helvetica for it’s versatility and boldness. I decided on green as my accent color to represent vibrance and expressive energy. The paint splatter shape I feel captivates my originality and creativity. Lastly, to convey my deep knowledge of design and show people an underlying structure to my creativity, I decided to add contrast to my loose paint splatter silhouette by placing an orderly “B” at the focal point of my visual marker.
