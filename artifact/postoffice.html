Post Office Print Proposal

Iwas tasked with designing a proposal to sell upper management the idea that my college’s University Post office should have expanded responsibilities and should offer new products and services. While upper management viewed the proposal, it needed to be presented from a student’s perspective, so I designed it to resemble a handout that a student would have been given during orientation.

...I had to make quick decisions that all had real world consequences.
I was only given 2 hours to write, design, and implement the entire project, and had to make quick decisions that all had real world consequences. I hand sketched thumbnails to choose a layout. My writing voice was casual and snarky so it would appeal to students(similar to Spirit Airlines). I chose a sophisticated typeface combo: Helvetica Bold for headlines, and Garamond for body text. The graphic was an open source image that I edited heavily in photoshop for improved color and contrast. The design was successful as upper management approved the idea.
