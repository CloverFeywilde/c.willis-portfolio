Post Office Print Proposal

Iworked full time at the university’s post office all through my college experience, and used my visual design skills to help improve our exposure and visibility. One such project assigned to me was to design an attractive proposal to sell the idea to upper management that the post office should have expanded responsibilities and offer more products and services. The main challenge of this design problem was targeting the correct audience. The audience for the proposal was obviously upper management, but the assignment was to sell the idea to upper management from a student’s perspective. Therefore, I was to design the flyer to make it resemble a document that a student would have been handed during orientation.

...I had to make quick decisions that all had real world consequences.
I was only given 2 hours to write, design, and implement the entire project, so I had to make quick decisions that all had real world consequences. I started with just pen and paper by making a few thumbnails. I played with symmetry and contrast until I came up with a general design I liked. I then wrote the verbiage for the document and chose a writing voice that was witty, common-sense, and casual. (Similar to what Spirit Airlines uses for their branding) The typography I approached carefully, for if I chose too free spirited of a typeface for the body text, I would risk turning off the upper management looking over the proposal. For this reason I went with a sophisticated and classically designed typeface combo, Helvetica Bold for headlines, and Garamond for body text. The graphic was an open source design that I edited heavily in photoshop to change it’s colors and contrast.
