Interactive Game Design Document

The purpose of this website was to take a lengthy and hard to read game design document, and converting it to a format that’s easier on the eyes and more presentable for potential backers, collaborators, and/or publishers. The obvious design problem here was working with unformatted walls of text and styling them for easy readability. I decided to go with a simple 1-page design, and a scrolling navbar so users can easily access specific pieces of information. My process involved: tagging the document properly with HTML, styling it with CSS, and using wordpress to streamline the development process.

The contents of document, including the illustrations, were all written, created, and designed by myself.
I ended up having to alter the theme I used quite a bit to fit my needs. As far as the design is concerned, I broke up the document into relevant sections, then further broke each section into readable blocks of text. I adjusted the line length to around 70-80 characters for optimum readability, added drop caps and some of my concept illustrations to create multiple entry points, and chose an easy on the eyes typeface, Lato, as my body text. The contents of document, including the illustrations, were all written, created, and designed by myself. The title of the game is “Faerie Heart”, and it’s an original RPG styled fantasy video game.
