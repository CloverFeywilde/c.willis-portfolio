Model Photo Sale Advertisement

Amodel that I typically shoot wanted a social media advertisement she could use to help strengthen her brand and give her a high amount of views and likes over facebook. I came up with suggestion that she could add a unique a personal flair to her business by selling her customers one of a kind, individually signed polaroid photos.

The result would be her presenting herself a unique and “authentic” light as a model, and the customers feeling more involved with her work. To advertise her photo sale, we traveled to south Georgia and did a photoshoot of her on a private farm during the height of fall. We used up nearly 3 packs of polaroid film and shot her in an array of poses and different settings on the property. The challenge of this design problem was conveying the unique vintage qualities of a personal polaroid photo, while keeping the image crisp and clean. To remedy this, I used mirrorless high end sony camera body paired with a few vintage lenses from the 1980’s. The resulting images left me with photographs that were both vibrant and crisp, but that had a unique blurring effect. We hand painted some apple crates for her to pose on for the shoot, and decided for the advertisement photo to reuse those as a repeating element. While this design problem isn’t the typical setting that a visual designer finds themselves in, it required and benefitted from my design skills, as elements of repetition, contrast, alignment, asymmetry, and more were employed.
