Skatergirl
Living in a world filled with images, ideas, and symbols, I can no longer determine what’s real and what’s not. The concept of tangible and intangible grows hazier by the day, as mere ideas have grown more importance and weight than the physical object that those ideas represent. So which is more real? The girl, or the boy?

This piece started off as an exercise for me to draw a male and a female side by side, and turned into a conceptual thought experiment. In this illustration, the girl is standing in front of the wall behind her, and she’s standing next to a boy that she’s painted herself. Between the grafitti on the wall, the branding on the girl’s board, and the painting of the boy behind her, the piece explores the idea of “representation” and blurs what the viewer interprets as “real” and “not real”. The main challenge to this piece was the amount of research that needed to go into it. I had to research how graffiti artist from the 70’s - 90’s created and designed their respective tags. In addition, the board the girl’s holding was drafted in photoshop so it would remain neat, orderly, and hard edged.
